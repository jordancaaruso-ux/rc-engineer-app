## Camber

**Keys:** `camber_front`, `camber_rear` — camber is the **tilt of the tire relative to the ground** (angle in the wheel plane).

**More camber** can **increase side bite** and peak grip in many conditions, but past the sweet spot **grip can fall off more sharply** when you exceed what the tire can use. **Less camber** often gives **less peak grip** overall but a **more progressive** feel as you approach the limit.

**Balance:** Adding camber **to one end** tends to **add grip at that end** and shift **mechanical balance** — often felt strongly **off throttle** (braking / entry). **On throttle**, especially with **more camber**, the car can be **harder to manage** in **higher-powered** classes because the loaded tire is working in a narrower window.

## Caster (front vs rear)

**Keys:** `caster_front`, `caster_rear` — **front and rear caster behave very differently**; do not treat them as the same knob.

**Front caster:** **More front caster** often **smooths initial turn-in** but can give **more steering** **mid-corner and on exit** (and the **reverse** when you reduce it). **More caster** can also make the car **harder to drive on throttle** in some setups — tires, grip, and power level matter.

**Rear caster:** Often acts partly like a **small wheelbase** change. It also **interacts with rear toe gain** — if you change rear caster, you may need to **re-trim toe / toe-gain shims** so the rear behaves as intended. **More negative rear caster** (more negative angle in the convention your sheet uses) often **adds rotation**; drivers often run **more negative** on **small tracks with many 180° corners**. **Flowing** tracks often move **toward less negative** rear caster for stability and drive off corners.

## Front toe

**Key:** `toe_front` — in the usual convention where **more negative** means **more toe-in** (check your setup sheet): **more front toe (more negative)** tends to make the car **less edgy** initially — **smoother** turn-in feel. **Less front toe** (closer to zero or toe-out, depending on sign convention) tends to feel **sharper** and more **immediate** on entry. Confirm **sign convention** on your car before comparing numbers to other drivers.

## Rear toe

**Key:** `toe_rear` — **more rear toe** (typically more rear toe-in in the usual convention) usually **increases rear grip** at the cost of **rotation** and sometimes **corner speed**. It often feels **safer and easier**, especially **mid-corner to exit**. **Less rear toe** usually gives **more rotation** and **less rear grip**; the car is often **harder to drive** and less forgiving. Match changes to tires, surface grip, and whether you need stability or rotation.
