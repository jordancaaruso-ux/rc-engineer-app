## Bump steer (front)

**Key:** `bump_steer_shims_front` — bump steer shimming on the **front** of the car.

**Convention (this platform):** **More shims → more bump-in** on compression. **Fewer shims → more bump-out** on compression.

With **more bump-in**, the **outer** wheel in a corner, as it compresses and loads, tends to **add steer** (toe-in through travel): the car can feel **more aggressive initially**, with **more initial steering** and bite. Depending on where you started, that often makes the car **edgier and harder to drive**, but if the car was **very lazy** it can also feel **more precise**.

**Throttle / extension:** Behaviour **reverses** when load comes off the outer tire. If the car has **bump-in on compression**, **unloading** that outer wheel (e.g. on throttle) tends toward **bump-out**, so the car can **want to go straighter off the corner**. If the setup has **bump-out on compression**, **extension** can go toward **bump-in**, which can feel like **more turn on throttle** off the corner.

You **cannot know the true bump steer curve** from shims alone — **measure** toe at **ride height** vs **full compression** (and how much toe changes per mm travel) if you need numbers. **Roughly neutral** is a common baseline; many cars run deliberate bump-in or bump-out for the handling they want.

## Toe gain (rear)

**Key:** `toe_gain_shims_rear` — toe gain shimming on the **rear** of the car.

**Convention (this platform):** **More shims → more bump-out / toe loss on compression**. **Fewer shims → more bump-in / toe gain on compression** (more rear toe-in as the wheel compresses).

**More toe gain** (more rear toe-in in bump) usually **adds rear grip**, especially **mid-corner through exit**, when the **outer** rear is compressed and carries more toe-in. Drivers often report **more overall rear grip**, a **more stable** and **easier** car, and a tendency toward **understeer** from **mid-corner to exit**, **especially on throttle** — platform and tires matter; **test on track**.

Sign of toe gain vs static rear toe depends on **link geometry**; shims describe the **bump curve**, not necessarily static toe at ride height alone.
