## Initial vs overall grip — what you’re choosing

**Initial grip** is how hard the car bites **on entry**: peak response, aggression, a **high peak** of grip then often a **drop** as roll builds. **Overall grip** is how **consistent** grip feels through the corner: smoother, more **constant**, the car **slower to react** and often easier to read at the limit. Geometry (upper link, RC height, hub trim) shifts you along this spectrum together with tires and surface—use this as a map, not a guarantee.

## Low RC and strong overall grip — entry and feel

A car with **low roll centre** and a lot of **overall** grip tends to **enter corners smoothly**—sometimes **too smooth**: it can feel **imprecise** or vague if you want a sharp attack. It rewards a **rolled-in**, **in the track** style rather than a snappy stab.

## High RC and strong initial grip — entry and aggression

A car with **high roll centre** and a lot of **initial** grip **enters aggressively**: very responsive, can feel **harder to drive** because it is **too reactive**. You get a **peak** of bite early, then the familiar **mid-corner** trade-offs from higher RC (see **Per end** theory in `roll-centre.md`).

## Mid-corner — overall vs initial personality

**Low RC** with **overall** grip: the car **maintains grip** mid-corner, **rotates by rolling**, and often feels **safe** and **in the track**. **High initial-grip** / higher-RC character: rotation can come from **letting go of rear grip**—can feel **edgy**, **catch bumps**, or **want to flip / tip** if you overdrive (jacking and bump sensitivity matter).

## Corner exit — fishtail vs drive away

With a lot of **overall** grip and **low RC** tendency, **exit** can **fishtail** or feel **unstable and imprecise**—like the car **won’t want to drive straight** on throttle. **High RC** with strong **initial** grip often **wants to go straight off the corner** with good bite; it can also **snap** or **lose grip quickly** if **initial** bite is too much or timing is wrong.

## Under-hub shims — trimming initial vs overall

**Keys:** `under_hub_shims_front`, `under_hub_shims_rear`. After upper link and inner lower arm, use hub height to trim: **lower** hub stack → more **initial** grip; **higher** stack → more **overall** grip (see `roll-centre.md`). Combine with RC balance—**initial vs overall** is not the same as **front vs rear** balance, but both matter together.
