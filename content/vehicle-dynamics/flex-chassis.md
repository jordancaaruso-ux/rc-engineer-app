## Flex (general)

**More flex** usually makes the car **more forgiving** and can feel like **more overall grip**, especially when **track grip is low**. When **grip is high**, **too much flex** can feel like **less grip**: **vague**, lacking **support** and **precision**. Flex interacts with **springs, dampers, and geometry** — treat changes as part of a **whole car** balance.

## Front vs rear flex (handling)

**More front flex** tends to **calm initial steering** / feel **smoother on turn-in**, and can add **mid-corner steering** as the nose loads. **More rear flex** often **increases rotation**, especially **on throttle**. **Less rear flex** often **improves rear grip** and **reduces rotation** — but if the rear is **too stiff**, some drivers feel **more rotation on throttle** (unload / snap) depending on diff, springs, and surface. **Test on track.**

## Chassis material (carbon vs steel vs RAF)

**Key:** `chassis` — options vary by kit (e.g. carbon, steel, RAF-style plates).

**Carbon** is usually the **softest** chassis flavour: often the **most overall grip** in **low-grip** conditions and can be the **easiest** when grip is **low** or the surface is **bumpy / inconsistent**. **Steel** typically **lowers CoG** and has **less flex** than carbon; drivers often use **softer top decks** or other flex tools to **reintroduce** compliance. **RAF / stiff alloy-type** plates are often the **stiffest**: if **stiffness is too high** for the conditions, the car can **lack overall grip** and **rotation**; if you still have **too much flex** in the stack for **high grip**, it can feel **imprecise**. **Verify** exact part numbers and flex character with your **manufacturer** sheet.

## Top deck: C127 vs C127S (example)

**Keys:** `top_deck_front`, `top_deck_rear` — common presets include **C127** and **C127S** (names from setup sheets). **C127** is often **slightly wider** and therefore **stiffer** than **C127S** in many kits; use your **manual** for the exact part you run.

## Top deck screws and motor mount screws (stiffness)

**Keys:** `top_deck_screws`, `motor_mount_screws` — more screws engaged usually means **more stiffness** in that zone.

- **Top deck screws A–B** (front pair): adding screws here typically adds **front** stiffness.
- **Top deck screws E–F** (rear pair): adding screws here typically adds **rear** stiffness.
- **Motor mount screws** positions **1–2**: often associated with **front** stiffness; positions **3–4–5** with **rear** stiffness (layout is kit-specific — match labels on **your** car).

## Top deck cuts (flex)

**Key:** `top_deck_cuts` — cuts usually **add flex** in the region of the deck.

- Cuts **A–D** (front half of the deck grid): usually **front flex**.
- Cuts **E–H** (rear half): usually **rear flex**.

Use the **same A–H grid** your PDF / app uses; do not assume letters match another brand’s deck.
