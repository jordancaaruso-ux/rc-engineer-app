## Grip and mechanical balance

Low-grip surfaces often reward a slightly freer car: less aggressive spring rates so the tire can work without overloading. High-grip tracks can tolerate more spring and damping to keep the platform stable through direction changes.

## Understeer mid-corner

If the car pushes after turn-in, first confirm entry speed and line. Mechanically, a small reduction in front roll stiffness or more rear traction bias (within rules) often helps. Make one logical change at a time and verify over several laps.

## High-speed understeer

Often aero or damper-related: the nose may not stay loaded in sustained corners. Consider small high-speed damping or balance shifts before large spring changes.
