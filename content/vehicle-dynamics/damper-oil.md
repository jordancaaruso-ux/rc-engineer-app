## Damper oil (thicker vs lighter)

**Keys:** `damper_oil_front`, `damper_oil_rear` — **thicker oil** increases **damping** for a given piston / build (exact feel depends on **pistons, holes, and spring rate**).

**Thicker oil (front and rear)** usually feels **less reactive**, **easier to drive**, and often **better over bumps** / rough track — the car **moves less** on quick inputs and surface hits.

**Thicker front oil** tends to **calm initial steering** and **remove some mid-corner rotation** while feeling **smoother** and **easier** overall. **Thicker rear oil** is broadly similar in character: often **less rotation**, **easier**, **more compliant** in many conditions.

**Thicker oil** can give the **feeling of more overall grip** because the platform is more **controlled**. **Lighter oil** often feels like **more initial grip** / bite because the car **reacts faster** and loads the tire more abruptly — which can also feel **edgier**. Tune **oil together with springs and pistons**; do not change only oil in isolation on a balanced car.
