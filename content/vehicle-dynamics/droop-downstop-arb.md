## Droop and downstop (wheel travel)

**Keys:** `droop_front`, `droop_rear`, `downstop_front`, `downstop_rear` — sheets differ in whether **droop** (allowed extension) and **downstop** (travel limit) are separate or combined; read the **column label** on your setup. **Convention here:** a **lower number** with **more droop** means the car can **extend the wheels further** while still keeping **tire contact** over bumps and in roll (more available **extension** travel).

**Front:** **More front droop** (lower number / more extension in your sheet’s convention) often adds **mid-corner steering** and **exit steering**. **Too much** can feel **floaty**, especially **on throttle**. **Too little** front droop tends to feel **stiff near the limit**, **scrub** at the **apex**, and give **less mid-corner steering** and **less steering on throttle**.

**Rear:** **Less rear droop** (tighter extension / less travel — direction per sheet) often **reduces steering from entry through mid-corner**. **More rear droop** tends to do the **opposite** (more rotation through that phase). Confirm **raise/lower vs more/less travel** on your car’s manual.

## ARB / sway bar (front)

**Key:** `arb_front` — **softer** front ARB / **thinner** bar: often **more mid-corner steering**, especially in **hairpins**; can **smooth** initial front grip and feel **less grabby** / easier to drive. **Stiffer** front ARB: often **more initial bite** and **more steering in the first part** of the corner, then **more push / understeer** **later** in the same corner as the front loads up.

## ARB / sway bar (rear)

**Key:** `arb_rear` — rear ARB is often **less predictable** than front. **Stiffer** rear can **add rotation under high load**, especially **on throttle**, but **too stiff** can **remove rotation in hairpins** because the **rear cannot roll enough** to work the tire. **Softer** rear can sometimes give **more mid-corner grip**, but also **less stability under high load**; it can **add rotation in hairpins** depending on balance. **Test** and match to surface grip and driving style.
